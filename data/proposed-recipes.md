# 20 Proposed New Tone Recipes

Curated list of next-priority artist tones to add to the catalog. Each entry includes a brief gear sketch + the verified Helix model that fits + a research priority rating.

Priority rubric:
- ⭐⭐⭐ — iconic tone with well-documented gear, dual-mic capable, will rebuild cleanly with current generator

## High-priority (⭐⭐⭐) — clean rebuilds

### 1. Eddie Van Halen — Eruption (Brown Sound)
- **Gear**: Frankenstrat → variac'd Marshall Plexi 1959 → 4x12 Greenback / Celestion Blue
- **Helix**: Brit Plexi Brt + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan) + EVH Phaser
- **Why**: The most-asked-about tone in rock guitar. Half the magic is the variac (we'll simulate via Master + Sag).

### 2. SRV — Texas Flood (slow-blues lead)
- **Gear**: #1 Strat → TS808 → cranked Vibroverb → Super Reverb 4x10
- **Helix**: Scream 808 + US Deluxe Vib + 4x10 Tweed P10R (WithPan)
- **Why**: Pairs with our Pride and Joy preset to demonstrate the dynamic range of one rig.

### 3. Iommi — Iron Man / Sweet Leaf (Black Sabbath doom riff)
- **Gear**: Detuned SG → Treble Booster → Laney Supergroup
- **Helix**: Deranged Master + Brit J45 Brt + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Foundational doom/metal tone. C# tuning, treble-booster-into-Brit recipe.

### 4. Hetfield — Master of Puppets (rhythm)
- **Gear**: ESP Explorer → TS808 → Mesa Mark IIC+ → Marshall 4x12
- **Helix**: Scream 808 + Cali IV Lead + 4x12 Cali V30 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Definitive thrash rhythm tone. TS808 boost into a high-gain Mark series amp.

### 5. Hammett — Fade to Black (clean → wah → solo)
- **Gear**: ESP/Gibson → wah → Mesa → 4x12
- **Helix**: Multi-snapshot — clean US Deluxe + Cali Texas Ch1 → solo Cali IV Lead with wah
- **Why**: Showcases snapshot capability for a single song with three tonal sections.

### 6. Murray — The Trooper (Iron Maiden galloping lead)
- **Gear**: Strat with humbucker → Tube Screamer → Marshall JCM800
- **Helix**: Scream 808 + Brit 2204 + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Classic NWOBHM lead tone. Tighter than Slash, more midrange than Hammett.

### 7. Rhoads — Crazy Train (lead)
- **Gear**: Polka-dot V → MXR Distortion+ → Marshall JCM50 → 4x12
- **Helix**: Distortion+ + Brit Trem Brt + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Bridge between Page-era Marshall and modern metal. Distortion+ is verified in inventory.

### 8. EVH — Panama (Brown Sound)
- **Gear**: Wolfgang/EVH 5150III → 4x12
- **Helix**: PV Panama + 4x12 Cali V30 (WithPan)
- **Why**: 5150 era of Eddie's tone vs. the variac'd Plexi era — pairs with Eruption recipe.

### 9. Greenwood — Creep (clean → crunch transition)
- **Gear**: Tele/Strat → Big Muff → Vox AC30 (clean) / Marshall (crunch)
- **Helix**: Triangle Fuzz default-off + Essex A30 → snapshot to Brit Plexi for chorus
- **Why**: Demonstrates clean-to-explosive transition. The "scrunch scrunch" trick.

### 10. Lifeson — Tom Sawyer (chorus + solo)
- **Gear**: Gibson/Hentor Sportscaster → Hiwatt → 4x12
- **Helix**: WhoWatt 100 + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan) + Boss CE-2 chorus
- **Why**: Hiwatt + chorus is underrepresented in the catalog. Great Rush atmosphere.

## Medium-priority (⭐⭐) — usable now, may need follow-up

- **Helix**: Optical Trem + Brit J45 Brt + 4x12 Greenback 25
- **Why**: Iconic tremolo passage. Studio tremolo is hard to replicate exactly.

### 12. Townshend — Won't Get Fooled Again (chunky rhythm)
- **Gear**: SG → Hiwatt → 4x12 with Fane speakers
- **Helix**: WhoWatt 100 + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Pairs with our Gilmour Hiwatt presets but with humbuckers + power chords.

### 13. Richards — Start Me Up (open-G riff)
- **Gear**: Tele in open-G (5-string) → Mesa or Ampeg combo
- **Helix**: Cali Texas Ch1 + 1x12 US Deluxe (WithPan)
- **Why**: Open-G tuning is a learning moment for many guitarists. The amp is just clean.

### 14. Garcia — Truckin' (Grateful Dead clean sparkle)
- **Gear**: Wolf/Tiger custom → Twin Reverb → JBL speakers
- **Helix**: US Double Nrm + 2x12 Double C12N (WithPan)
- **Why**: Crystal-clean Twin tone with a touch of envelope filter for solos.

### 15. Santana — Smooth (lead)
- **Gear**: PRS → Mesa Boogie Mark series → 1x12 + Roland JC-120 in stereo
- **Helix**: Cali IV Lead + 2x12 Jazz Rivet (WithPan) — JC-120 stereo blend
- **Why**: The infinite-sustain Latin singing-tone trick. Jazz Rivet is verified.

### 16. Auerbach — Lonely Boy (Black Keys raw garage)
- **Gear**: Custom guitar → Big Muff Pi or Univox Super-Fuzz → cranked Marshall
- **Helix**: Triangle Fuzz + Brit Plexi Brt + 4x12 Greenback 25 (WithPan)
- **Why**: Lo-fi garage rock — fuzz + cranked Marshall, no compression.

### 17. Bonamassa — Sloe Gin (slow-blues lead)

- **Helix**: Scream 808 + Derailed Ingrid + 2x12 Mail C12Q (WithPan)

### 18. White — Seven Nation Army (the riff)
- **Gear**: Airline → DigiTech Whammy (octave down) → Big Muff → Fender Twin
- **Helix**: Pitch Wham (1 octave down) + Triangle Fuzz + US Double Nrm + 2x12 Double C12N (WithPan)
- **Why**: That bass-line guitar trick. Whammy + fuzz is the technique.

### 19. Homme — No One Knows (QOTSA detuned desert)
- **Gear**: Maton or Ovation → Ampeg VT-22 → tuned to C standard
- **Helix**: PV Panama + 4x12 Cali V30 (WithPan) — slightly less gain than Dimebag
- **Why**: Desert rock tone — detuned, slightly fuzzy, midrange-heavy.

### 20. Gallagher — Wonderwall (jangly rhythm)
- **Gear**: Epiphone EJ-200 acoustic + Stratocaster → Vox AC30
- **Helix**: Acoustic Sim (for capo'd intro) + Essex A30 + 2x12 Blue Bell (WithPan)
- **Why**: Unique acoustic + amp blend. Acoustic Sim is verified in inventory.
