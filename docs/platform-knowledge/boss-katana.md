## Product Line Overview

The Boss Katana is a line of modeling guitar amplifiers introduced in 2016 by Boss (a division of Roland Corporation). Over one million units have been sold across three generations, making it one of the most successful amplifier lines in history.

### Timeline

| Generation | Year | Key Milestone |
|------------|------|---------------|
| MkI | 2016 | Original launch. 5 amp types, 55 effects, Tube Logic design, Class AB power amp |
| MkII | 2019 | Refinements to bring entry-level models closer to Artist-level features. 60+ effects |
| Gen 3 | 2024 | Major overhaul. New Tube Logic engine, 6th amp type (Pushed), USB-C, built-in IR, Bloom circuit |

Boss deliberately named the third generation "Gen 3" rather than "MkIII" to signal that this was a more fundamental redesign, not just an iterative update. Notably, MkI and MkII patches and livesets remain compatible with Gen 3.

## Generation Comparison

### MkI (2016)

- 5 amp types: Acoustic, Clean, Crunch, Lead, Brown
- 55 effects accessible via Tone Studio
- 3 simultaneous effects
- USB Type-B connectivity
- Power Control (variable wattage)
- Class AB analog power amp
- Custom-designed speakers
- 4 Tone Setting memories (Katana-50), 8 memories (Katana-100 and above)

### MkII (2019)

- Same 5 amp types, refined voicings
- 60+ effects via Tone Studio
- 5 simultaneous effects (up from 3)
- Improved feature parity across models (entry-level closer to Artist)
- Enhanced EQ options
- Tone Setting memories expanded
- Still USB Type-B

### Gen 3 (2024)

- 6 amp types (added Pushed), each with a Variation = 12 amp voicings
- 60+ effects via Tone Studio (refined algorithms)
- 5 simultaneous effects
- USB-C connectivity
- Built-in Impulse Response (IR) technology for recording/headphones
- Bloom circuit (Head and Artist models)
- Cabinet Resonance selector (Katana-100 and above)
- Solo Boost button (Katana-50 EX and above)
- Contour 3-way switch (Katana-100 and above)
- Optional Bluetooth via BT-DUAL adapter (sold separately, ~$60)
- Backward compatible with MkI/MkII patches and livesets

## Amp Types and Variations

### Front Panel Amp Types

All Katana models (Gen 3) have 6 selectable amp characters on the front panel, each with a Variation toggle for a total of 12 voicings:

| Amp Type | Description | Likely Inspiration |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Acoustic** | Full-range, flat response for acoustic-electric guitars | No amp modeling (FRFR) |
| **Clean** | Sparkly, chimey clean tones | Roland JC-120 / Fender Blackface |
| **Crunch** | Medium-gain, classic rock breakup | Fender Tweed / early Marshall |
| **Pushed** (NEW in Gen 3) | Edge-of-breakup, touch-responsive saturation | Driven clean combo into harmonic saturation |
| **Lead** | High-gain, modern lead voicing | Peavey 5150 / modern high-gain (Mesa, Soldano, etc.) |
| **Brown** | Thick, saturated '80s rock gain | Eddie Van Halen "Brown Sound" / hot-rodded Marshall |

The **Variation** button on each amp type provides an alternate voicing. For example, the Clean variation might shift from a JC-120-style tone toward a warmer Fender-style character. This effectively doubles the amp selection from 6 to 12 without menu diving.

### MkI / MkII Amp Types (5 total)

The original five amp types (Acoustic, Clean, Crunch, Lead, Brown) have been present since MkI. The MkII refined their voicings and added the Variation toggle. Gen 3 added the Pushed type and further refined all existing types.

## Sneaky Amps

### What Are Sneaky Amps?

The Katana's digital engine shares DNA with the Boss GT-100 multi-effects processor. When Boss programmed the Katana, the COSM amp modeling code from the GT-100 platform was loaded onto the Katana's internal board. This means there are actually **28 amp models** inside the Katana, though only 5 (MkI/MkII) or 6 (Gen 3) are accessible from the front panel.

The additional "hidden" amp types are commonly called **Sneaky Amps** in the community. Boss has acknowledged their existence but notes they are not optimized for the Katana's power amp section the way the stock models are.

**Important:** Boss has never officially confirmed what specific real-world amps each model is based on. The identifications below are based on community consensus from GT-100 documentation and player experience.

### How to Access Sneaky Amps

Sneaky Amps can be loaded via community-created livesets imported through Boss Tone Studio. The katana-dev community on GitHub maintains a liveset package containing all Sneaky Amps (including Bright variants, totaling 30 patches).

They are NOT accessible from the amp's front panel or through any official Boss Tone Studio menu. You must use third-party SysEx tools or pre-built livesets.

## Signal Flow and Effects Chain

### Default Signal Chain

The Katana's internal signal chain follows this general order:

```
Guitar Input
  -> Booster
  -> [Pre-amp Effects Position A]
  -> Amp Preamp (Tube Logic modeling)
  -> [Post-amp Effects Position B]
  -> EQ Section
  -> [Effects Loop Send/Return position]
  -> Power Amp (Class AB analog)
  -> Speaker / Line Out / USB Out
```

### Chain Customization via Tone Studio

Boss Tone Studio provides a **Chain** screen where you can rearrange the position of each effect block relative to the preamp. Effects can be placed:

- **Before the preamp** (typical for: booster, wah, compressor, overdrive)
- **After the preamp** (typical for: modulation, delay, reverb)

The chain has three configurable positions. Tone Studio shows the complete signal path visually and lets you drag effects between pre and post positions.

### Effects Loop

Available on: Katana-50 EX, Katana-100, Katana-100/212, Katana Head, Katana Artist, Katana Artist Head.

The effects loop is located within the amplifier's EQ section of the signal chain by default. Via Tone Studio, you can adjust:

- **Chain position:** Move the loop insert point between Chain 1, Chain 2, or Chain 3 positions
- **Series vs Parallel operation:** Toggle between series (100% wet signal passes through loop) and parallel (blend original signal with loop signal)
- **Send/Return levels:** Fine-tune the signal level going to and coming from external effects

**Best practices:**
- For time-based effects (delay, reverb, loopers): Place in the effects loop (post-preamp)
- For gain-based effects (overdrive, boost, wah): Place in front of the amp input
- For looper pedals (RC-3, RC-30, RC-300): Use Chain 3 position for best results

### GA-FC Foot Controller

The Boss GA-FC (sold separately) provides hands-free control:

- 6 footswitches for toggling individual effects on/off (stompbox-style)
- Channel preset switching (switch between 4 Tone Settings)
- 2 additional inputs for external footswitches or expression pedals
- Expression pedal assignment to any parameter (wah, volume, delay feedback, etc.)
- Can be supplemented with FS-5U footswitch and EV-30 expression pedal

The GA-FC effectively turns the Katana into a full pedalboard-and-amp rig.

Available on: Katana-50 EX and above (all Gen 3 models except the base Katana-50).

### MIDI Control

The Katana supports MIDI input for:

- Bank and channel switching
- Effect parameter control (CC messages)
- Preset recall
- Default MIDI channel: 1 (configurable by holding a Channel button during power-on)

MIDI can be received via:
- The BT-DUAL Bluetooth adapter (wireless MIDI)
- Direct MIDI connection (Gen 3 models)

The System screen in Tone Studio provides access to MIDI, USB, Line Out, and footswitch configuration.

## Boss Tone Studio

### Overview

Boss Tone Studio (BTS) is the free desktop application (macOS and Windows) that unlocks the full potential of the Katana amplifier. It is also available as a mobile app for iOS and Android when using the optional BT-DUAL Bluetooth adapter.

### What Tone Studio Unlocks (vs Front Panel Only)

| Capability | Front Panel | Tone Studio |
|-----------|:-----------:|:-----------:|
| Amp type selection | 6 types (Gen 3) | 6 types + variations |
| Effect selection | 3 per category (15 total) | 60+ total effects |
| Effect parameters | Basic (1-2 knobs per effect) | Full parameter sets (5-15 params per effect) |
| Chain/signal flow editing | No | Yes (drag and drop) |
| Effects loop configuration | No | Series/Parallel, level, position |
| IR/cab sim editing | No | Yes (mic type, position, ambience) |
| Sneaky Amps loading | No | Via liveset import |
| Liveset management | No | Yes (organize, import, export) |
| Tone Exchange access | No | Yes (browse/download community presets) |
| Global EQ editing | No | Yes |
| MIDI configuration | No | Yes |
| USB recording settings | No | Yes |

### Key Tone Studio Features

**Editor Screen:** Full visual interface showing every parameter for the currently loaded tone. All knobs, sliders, and switches are adjustable in real-time. Changes are heard immediately through the amp.

**Chain Screen:** Visual signal flow diagram. Drag and drop effects to rearrange the signal chain. Move effects between pre-amp and post-amp positions.

**Librarian:** Organize Tone Settings into banks. Import/export livesets. Drag tones between memory positions on the amp.

**IR Designer (Gen 3):** Configure the built-in impulse response for recording and headphone output. Select cabinet type, microphone model, mic position, and ambience level. Note: you cannot upload your own third-party IRs.

**Send/Return Configuration:** Adjust effects loop level, position, and series/parallel mode.

**System Settings:** Configure MIDI channel, USB audio routing, line out behavior, and footswitch assignments.

### Boss Tone Exchange

Boss Tone Exchange is the community preset-sharing platform. Accessible directly from within the Tone Studio app (Gen 3). Users can:

- Browse and download free presets/livesets created by other users
- Upload and share their own presets
- Filter by amp model, genre, or artist style
- Download official Boss and artist-curated tone patches from Tone Central

### Connecting to Tone Studio

1. Download Boss Tone Studio from boss.info
2. Connect Katana to computer via USB cable (USB-C for Gen 3, USB-B for older models)
3. Launch Tone Studio; it auto-detects the connected amp
4. All changes made in Tone Studio are reflected on the amp in real-time
5. Save tones to amp memory or to your computer library

## Power Control

### How It Works

The Power Control knob on the rear panel adjusts the Katana's power amplifier output stage. This is not a simple volume reducer; it is a power scaling system that adjusts how the Class AB power amp operates, affecting headroom, breakup characteristics, and overall dynamics.

### Available Settings by Model

| Model | Settings |
|-------|---------|
| Katana-50 | Standby / 0.5W / 25W / 50W |
| Katana-100, Head, Artist | Standby / 0.5W / 50W / 100W |

### What Each Setting Does

| Setting | Use Case | Behavior |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Standby** | Set breaks, silence | Disables the internal speaker entirely. Signal still passes to Line Out/USB |
| **0.5W** | Bedroom/apartment practice | Minimum volume. Power amp saturates easily for natural compression at whisper levels |
| **25W** (50W models) | Home practice, small rooms | Moderate headroom and volume |
| **50W** | Rehearsal, small to mid gigs | Good headroom for clean sounds, natural power amp breakup at higher volumes |
| **100W** (100W models) | Full gigs, maximum headroom | Maximum clean headroom and volume. Requires significant volume to drive the power amp into saturation |

### Key Characteristics

- **Tone preservation:** Boss designed the Power Control to maintain the same tonal character across all wattage settings. A Brown tone at 0.5W should sound characteristically similar to Brown at 100W, just at a different volume
- **Headroom changes:** Lower wattage = less headroom = earlier power amp breakup. This can be used creatively for driven tones at lower volumes
- **Dynamic response:** The Class AB power amp's dynamic response scales with the wattage setting. Lower settings feel more compressed; higher settings feel more open and responsive

## Recording and Connectivity

### USB Audio Interface

All Katana models (50 and above) function as a USB audio interface. Connect via USB to your DAW and record directly without an external audio interface.

**Signal routing (MkII and Gen 3):**

| USB Channel | Signal |
|-------------|--------|
| Primary (1/2) | Wet signal: fully processed audio with amp modeling, EQ, and all effects |
| Secondary (3/4) | Dry/DI signal: raw guitar input before preamp and effects processing |

Recording both wet and dry simultaneously allows for re-amping the dry signal later using the Katana or software plugins.

**Gen 3 USB improvements:**
- USB-C connector (faster, more reliable)
- Built-in IR technology applied to USB output for more realistic cabinet simulation
- Improved latency

**Monitoring note:** You cannot monitor DAW output through the Katana's internal speakers when using it as a USB interface. DAW output monitoring is available only through the Phones/Rec Out jack using headphones.

### Line Out / Headphone Out / Rec Out

| Output | Available On | Cab Sim | Notes |
|--------|-------------|---------|-------|
| Phones/Rec Out (1/4") | All models | Yes | Built-in speaker emulation. Gen 3 adds IR technology |
| IR-Enhanced Line Out | Katana-50 EX and above | Yes (IR) | Improved cab simulation for direct-to-PA or recording |
| XLR Balanced Line Out | Artist Gen 3 only | Yes + Air Feel | Professional balanced output with mic placement and ambience simulation |
| External Speaker Out | Head models | No (raw signal) | For connecting external guitar cabinets |

### Air Feel (Artist Models)

The Air Feel feature on the Artist Gen 3 adds advanced mic placement and room ambience simulation to the XLR line output. This provides a more natural, "mic'd amp in a room" sound for live PA use or studio recording without actually miking the amp.

### IR (Impulse Response) Designer - Gen 3

All Gen 3 models include a built-in IR system for the USB, headphone, and line outputs:

- Select from Boss-provided cabinet models
- Choose microphone type
- Adjust mic position (distance and angle)
- Set ambience/room level
- Cannot load third-party IR files
- Cab Resonance (Vintage/Modern/Deep) can be combined with IR settings for additional tonal shaping

## Gen 3 Specific Features

### Tube Logic Engine (3rd Generation)

The Katana's core technology is Boss's Tube Logic design philosophy. Rather than modeling only the output characteristics of a tube amplifier, Tube Logic models the behavior of individual components within a tube amp circuit: preamp tubes, phase inverter, power tubes, output transformer, and speaker interaction.

**Gen 3 improvements:**
- Refined component modeling with greater detail and nuance
- More responsive dynamics and touch sensitivity
- Improved harmonic complexity, especially in the Brown amp type
- Paired with a reactive Class AB analog power section (not Class D)

### Pushed Amp Type (New)

The sixth amp character introduced in Gen 3. Pushed delivers the sound of a clean combo amplifier driven into harmonic saturation. Key characteristics:

- Touch-responsive gain: cleans up with lighter picking, breaks up with attack
- Edge-of-breakup territory that responds to guitar volume knob
- Designed for blues, country, classic rock, and dynamic playing styles
- Fills the gap between Clean and Crunch that many players requested

### Bloom Circuit

Available on: Katana Head Gen 3, Katana Artist Gen 3, Katana Artist Head Gen 3.

A dedicated analog circuit engaged via a front-panel switch. Bloom introduces:

- Sharper note attack with enhanced transient response
- Richly dynamic low-end response
- A fundamentally different amp feel (not just an EQ change)
- Designed to make the amp respond more like a cranked tube amp at any volume

### Cabinet Resonance Selector

Available on: Katana-100 Gen 3 and above.

A 3-position switch that changes the speaker interaction modeling:

| Setting | Character |
|---------|-----------|
| Vintage | Looser, warmer low-end. Classic rock feel |
| Modern | Tighter, more controlled low-end. Modern rock/metal |
| Deep | Extended bass response. Full, thick tone |

### Solo Boost

Available on: Katana-50 EX Gen 3 and above.

A dedicated button that engages a pre-configured volume/gain boost for lead passages. On the Artist models, the Solo section includes both a level boost and a dedicated delay effect.

### Built-in IR Technology

All Gen 3 models include impulse response processing applied to:

- USB recording output
- Headphone/Phones output
- Line out (where equipped)

This ensures that direct recordings and silent practice sound like a properly mic'd cabinet rather than a raw preamp signal.

### Backward Compatibility

Gen 3 Katana amplifiers can load patches and livesets created for MkI and MkII models. This preserves the massive library of community presets available on Boss Tone Exchange and Tone Central.
