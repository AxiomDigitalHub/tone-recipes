### What F&K Does Consistently Well

- **Deep equipment archaeology.** The posts don't settle for "use a Big Muff"; they cite specific variants (Ram's Head vs. NYC reissue), production years, and circuit differences. Malcolm Young post, Comfortably Numb post, Loveless post all exceed typical gear guides in specificity.
- **Cross-platform translation.** Every modeler-focused post (QC presets, Misha djent, John Mayer on Helix/QC) provides Helix, Quad Cortex, and/or Axe-Fx equivalents. No "figure it out yourself" hand-waving.
- **Non-obvious technical insights.** Guitar volume at 7 not 10. Big Muff Sustain at 25-35% for vocal character. Klon Treble as harshness removal, not brightness control. Compression placement and ratio for djent density. These are not surface-level.
- **Pick technique and tone interaction.** Multiple posts (djent, Comfortably Numb, Loveless) explicitly address technique as tone source, not settings alone. Shows understanding of the physical domain beyond the signal chain.
- **Honest limitations.** Elena's "time is the variable" framing. Big Muff/Hiwatt "you can't replicate power tube compression at bedroom volumes" admission. Loveless "it's a four-track stereo record" reality check. Builds credibility.

### What F&K Does Consistently Poorly or Not at All

- **No preset product CTAs.** Of 12 posts, only three have any link to a preset library (/browse in Elena's post, /platforms/helix in Nathan's, /blog links in others). Zero posts end with "Now try this preset" or "Download the Misha djent preset." The SaaS backend exists but is almost completely invisible in editorial.

No verification of first-hand claims. Rick's "I tested Malcolm Young settings on a 1963 Gretsch" is absent; the post reads researched, not lived.
- **StoryBrand reader problem framing is buried or absent.** Most posts hook attention (Malcolm: "Everyone talks about Angus") but don't frame the reader's specific pain point. Elena nails this; others assume the reader already wants that tone. "Why does this matter to YOU?" is mostly missing.
- **Minimal internal linking strategy.** Posts link to other /blog posts ad hoc, but no strategic "next post" or "related tone recipe" navigation. Loveless post links to Nothing post; most stand alone. No content hub experience.
- **Video or audio anchors completely absent.** No embedded YouTube clips, no "here's what this tone sounds like" audio samples, no modeler preset walk-through videos. Competitors like Premier Guitar (Rig Rundowns on video) and That Pedal Show (video-native) own the multimodal trust domain.
- **Community or social proof nonexistent.** No reader comments visible (or disabled). No mention of "1000s of guitarists dialed this tone" or "community presets." Reverb News and Premier Guitar comments create belonging; F&K posts feel published into a void.
- **Artist partnership / first-person verification absent.** Neural DSP owns this (artist series collab). Premier Guitar owns Rig Rundowns (artist-verified). F&K's posts are well-researched but author-speculative. "Viktor says Misha uses this" vs. "Misha on video demonstrating this" — huge gap in E-E-A-T for modern SEO.
- **Newsletter lead capture UX not integrated.** No "subscribe for the next tone recipe" or "save this for later" conversion moment. Presets drive subscribers; presets aren't visible. The SaaS LTV opportunity is latent.

1. **Troubleshooting / "Why does my tone sound bad?" guides.** Competitors: "Why is my modeler tone fizzy?" (Neural DSP), "Fix fizzy high-gain" (specialized guides). F&K has /blog/why-modeler-tone-sounds-fizzy but it's an outlier. Need 5-10 more diagnostic posts.

9. **Frequency ranges and EQ education for non-technical players.** Posts mention "cut the harshness above 2kHz" or "low-pass filter at 6kHz" assuming reader knowledge. No dedicated "EQ frequencies explained" post. MusicRadar has this; F&K doesn't.

11. **Room acoustics and tone.** F&K hints at this (bedroom vs. stage volume). Zero dedicated "how your room affects your tone" or "home studio acoustic treatment" content. Sweetwater InSync has this; F&K doesn't.

12. **Cross-genre tone vocabulary / "what makes metal tone sound metallic?"** Most posts assume reader already knows the target tone. Need educational posts on tone families and characteristics.

## Executive Summary for Strategy Document

F&K has built an exceptionally well-researched editorial system. The technical depth, cross-platform translation, and non-obvious insights exceed most guitar publishing. The 10 named personas produce differentiated voices without requiring real humans.

However, the commercial layer is largely invisible. The SaaS backend (presets) is mentioned once (Elena's post) and never linked as a CTA. The E-E-A-T gap (no verified authors, no video, no artist partnerships) becomes more acute as search algorithms reward demonstrable expertise. The AEO readiness is 60 percent there (tables exist; schema doesn't).

The highest-leverage moves are not editorial — they're structural:

1. **Add FAQ schema** to existing FAQ blocks (5 min/post, 10 posts affected). Unlock featured snippet eligibility.
2. **Create preset CTAs** at the end of every tone recipe post. Link to the corresponding preset. This is the commercial bridge and the feature that differentiates F&K from BuzzFeed-style gear publishing.
3. **Verify one artist tone guide.** Contact a mid-tier artist (extended-range guitarist, worship leader, shoegaze-adjacent performer) and create a co-authored tone post. Breaks the AI-only perception immediately.
4. **Launch "Tone Demos" YouTube channel.** 10 min per tone: the persona plays the settings on video. Closes the trust gap and drives YouTube SEO. Even 1 video/week would reshape E-E-A-T perception.
5. **Position F&K as a preset platform, not a blog.** Reorganize the tone recipes to lead with "save this preset" before "here's the theory." The product should be the default next step, not an afterthought.

The editorial machine is working. The commercial machine is silent.
